Manhattan: The Diplomatic Center

Text, Maps and Photos by Shotaro Miyasaka | Aug 6, 2023 | Github | Back to Homepage

New York City is one of the most cosmopolitan cities in the world, attracting residents from all over the world. It is also home to the headquarters of the United Nations.

There are over 100 consulates general in Manhattan, New York.

A Cosmopolitan City Where Nations Meets

Strolling through the Midtown area in Manhattan, we can easily spot numerous buildings proudly flying national flags, as well as traffic signs designated for diplomatic vehicles. Occasionally, diplomatic cars can be seen cruising the streets.

There are over 100 consulates general representing various countries in New York City. The city has one of the highest concentrations of foreign missions outside of national capitals.

Many nations have their consulates in major urban centers to protect their citizens, conduct public diplomacy, and issue visas. The number of consulates reflects the attractiveness of the city.

Most of the consulates general in NYC are located in Manhattan, particularly in Midtown.

What are the characteristics of the location of these consulates general? So zoom in on the map of Manhattan.

Of the more than 100 consulates general in the city, over half are located within one kilometer of the UN headquarters. African and Middle Eastern countries have a significant presence in this area.

Nearly 80% are located within two kilometers.

Countries appear to prioritize access to business districts near the United Nations and Grand Central Station. Looking at the G7 countries, the consulates of Japan, Germany, the United Kingdom, and Canada are all located within one kilometer of the UN.

Locations of consulates also reveals the influence of historical relations and immigration patterns with the United States.

The consulates on the west side of Fifth Avenue in Midtown are mostly of Latin American countries. They are located near Penn Station and other busy areas.

The Upper East Side is home to a significant number of European consulates, including those of France and Italy, as the neighborhood has historically had a large European population.

Asian powers such as India, Pakistan, and Indonesia also have consulates in this area.

Consulates on the Upper East Side typically occupy freestanding buildings, most of which are low-rise structures.

In contrast, the consulates of countries such as Japan, the United Kingdom, Canada, Brazil and Australia, located in the bustling business district of Midtown, are tenants in large buildings and prioritize convenience for visitors and staff.

Consulates General of China and Russia, on the other hand, are located somewhat apart from those of other nations.The Russian consulate is located on 91st Street.

China's is located on 42nd Street near the Hudson River. China built on this site after the establishment of diplomatic relations between China and the U.S. in 1979, later than most other countries. The land would have been available at that time.

We sometimes see political messages near our diplomatic missions abroad. This big cosmopolitan city have something to do with international affairs either.
